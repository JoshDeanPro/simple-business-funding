# Step 4 — Messaging and Ethical Conversion

## Mission

Rewrite the content hierarchy so the website creates recognition, clarity, trust, and momentum without pressure, false urgency, or unsupported claims.

Do not implement the homepage until the messaging system is settled.

## Non-negotiable constraints

- Do not promise approval, funding, rates, speed, amounts, growth, reduced stress, family time, or life-changing outcomes.
- Do not imply SmallBizLoans is a bank, lender, or underwriter unless verified.
- Explain the relationship to Mom & Pop Business Funding accurately.
- Do not use desperation, fear, scarcity, countdowns, guilt, or manufactured urgency.
- Do not add fake testimonials, statistics, reviews, logos, or trust badges.
- Do not push to `main`.

## Core conversion model

Every page should support this sequence:

1. Recognition — “This relates to the problem I am trying to solve.”
2. Possibility — “Funding may help me address it.”
3. Clarity — “I understand what applying involves.”
4. Legitimacy — “I know who this company is and what it does.”
5. Manageable effort — “I know what I need and the process looks reasonable.”
6. Action — “I can begin without being trapped or misled.”

## Homepage message hierarchy

Define final copy for:

### Hero

- Outcome-led headline
- Short supporting explanation
- Primary application action
- Secondary preparation action
- One concise reassurance line
- Separate, readable disclosure

### Optional need recognition

Use one short optional question, not a quiz:

“What would you like your business to move forward on?”

Limit choices to practical business needs.

After selection, reflect one grounded statement using “may,” “could,” or “can help.”

Do not collect or score the selection.

### Business outcomes

Explain possible uses such as:

- Immediate operational need
- Inventory or equipment
- Growth opportunity
- Added capacity
- Operational breathing room

Connect them carefully to human priorities:

- Customers
- Employees
- Planning
- The work the owner built the business to do

Do not promise the outcome.

### Process

Explain the real process in three clear steps.

### Preparation

List only information actually needed.

### Role and trust

Explain:

- What SmallBizLoans does
- Its relationship to Mom & Pop Business Funding
- That it is not a bank
- Who reviews the application
- What happens after submission

### Human assistance

Use the real phone and email.

### Final action

Use one clear CTA with an accurate disclosure.

## Page-level copy rules

- Use plain English.
- Use active voice.
- Keep paragraphs short.
- Avoid internal financing jargon.
- Avoid “file coordination,” “application parameters,” and similar corporate phrases.
- Avoid generic AI phrases.
- Avoid rhetorical questions unless they genuinely help the visitor decide.
- Do not repeat the same promise on every page.
- Make button labels describe the actual action.

## Application microcopy

Define short explanations for:

- Before you begin
- Why ownership information is requested
- Why revenue information is requested
- Conditional prior-funding questions
- Documents
- Authorization
- Review
- Submission failure
- Submission success

Keep explanations useful, not defensive.

Do not proceed until:

- The business role is explained accurately
- The hero is benefit-led but not misleading
- The conversion path is clear
- The application requirements are understandable
- Unsupported claims have been removed
- Final copy is ready for implementation
