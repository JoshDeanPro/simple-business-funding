# Step 5 — Homepage Rebuild

## Mission

Rebuild the homepage using the approved architecture, research, design system, and messaging.

This is a full composition rebuild, not a restyling of existing sections.

## Non-negotiable constraints

- Keep the existing route and Worker behavior.
- Do not add backend features.
- Do not use a generic split hero with a plain rectangle image.
- Do not use a narrow centered document layout.
- Do not use a six-card feature grid.
- Do not use a large worksheet.
- Do not push to `main`.

## Required homepage sequence

1. Immersive financial-brand hero
2. Compact optional need-recognition launcher
3. Practical business outcomes
4. Connected application process
5. Preparation checklist
6. How SmallBizLoans helps
7. Human assistance
8. FAQ preview
9. Final application action
10. Footer

## Hero requirements

The hero must:

- Use the approved dark or branded color environment
- Have a confident, readable headline
- Use one primary CTA
- Use one quieter secondary action
- Keep disclosures separate from benefit statements
- Integrate authentic small-business photography
- Use depth, overlap, crop, or shape intentionally
- Transition naturally into the next section

The hero must not:

- Contain too many competing elements
- Use an invisible or low-contrast button
- Use a generic startup-office image
- Use a decorative fake product card
- Use fabricated numbers or claims
- Place a checklist and launcher and legal explanation all in the first viewport

## Need-recognition launcher

Use one optional question and no more than five choices.

Requirements:

- Choices, not numbered steps
- Clearly interactive
- Keyboard accessible
- One selection
- Skip allowed
- Not stored
- Not submitted
- Not used to imply eligibility
- Compact
- Visually connected to the hero

## Business outcomes

Use hierarchy rather than equal cards.

Possible composition:

- One featured outcome plus three supporting outcomes
- Two-by-two with different scale
- Editorial rows
- Image plus structured list

Avoid six identical tiles.

## Process

Create one connected three-stage workflow.

Use strong numbers, one shared track or background, concise copy, and a deliberate mobile stack.

## Preparation

Use one practical checklist.

Do not split preparation into three generic panels.

## Role explanation

Use the approved copy.

Present it confidently, not as buried fine print.

Do not place it before the visitor understands the value and process.

## Human assistance

Display the real contact details near a meaningful decision point.

Do not isolate phone and email in unrelated cards.

## FAQ preview

Use a small number of high-value questions.

Link to the full FAQ.

## Final CTA

Use a distinct but not generic black rounded slab.

The closing area should feel integrated with the overall design system.

## Photography

Use a suitable local, licensed, or generated image that matches the approved photography rules.

Do not hotlink production imagery.

Document the image source or generation method.

## Responsive behavior

Design mobile intentionally.

Verify:

- Headline wraps naturally
- Buttons remain visible
- Image crop remains meaningful
- Need choices are touch friendly
- No overlap covers text
- No horizontal overflow
- Section order remains logical

## Required deliverable

Create:

`docs/05-homepage-implementation.md`

Include:

- Sections implemented
- Components created or removed
- Image source
- Responsive decisions
- Any remaining content questions
- Before/after screenshot paths

## Completion gate

Do not proceed until:

- Homepage looks intentionally composed at desktop, tablet, and mobile
- Primary CTA is visible
- No generic startup-office image remains
- No section is a repeated card grid without a reason
- Need recognition is optional and compact
- All copy matches Step 4
- No Worker or form behavior changed
- Production build passes
